Dr. Prasad S. Thenkabail

     Dr. Prasad S. Thenkabail is currently working as a Research Geographer-15 with the U.S. Geological Survey (USGS), USA. Prasad is a well-recognized and respected international remote sensing scientist in areas of agriculture, water, food security, droughts, and resource management. He has conducted pioneering research in areas of: 1. hyperspectral remote sensing of vegetation, and 2. global croplands and their water use for food security. Recently, he led, as a principal investigator, the NASA and USGS funded seminal work on 30-m global cropland extent (Croplands; LPDAAC). He is currently the: 1. Editor-in-Chief of the Remote Sensing Open Access Journal published by MDPI, 2. Associate Editor of the Photogrammetric Engineering and Remote Sensing (PERS) of the American Society of Photogrammetry and Remote Sensing (ASPRS), and 3. Editorial Advisory Board, International Society of Photogrammetry and Remote Sensing (ISPRS) Journal of Photogrammetry and Remote Sensing. Earlier, he served on the editorial board of the Remote Sensing of Environment for many years. Recently in 2015, Dr. Thenkabail edited the three volume Remote Sensing Handbook (Publisher: Taylor and Francis Inc.) with 82 Chapters, and over 2000 pages with contribution from 300+ authors from around the World. Considered magnum opus on the subject the three-volume Remote Sensing Handbook is now considered a standard worldwide reference for remote sensing and spatial science professionals and students. In 2018, Prasad completed editing two volume Hyperspectral Remote Sensing of Vegetation (Publisher: Taylor and Francis Inc.). Prasad has 30+ years of work experience in 25+ countries spread across Africa, Asia, and Americas. He was selected by NASA and USGS as a member (2007-2011) of the prestigious Landsat Science Team. Prasad has over 140 peer-reviewed scientific publications, including several award winning papers, in the major International Journals. Prasad obtained his PhD from the Ohio State University in 1992.
